Look Into Your Expenses

At the start of each month, open up your checking account and review your expenses from the previous month. Classify each purchase as either necessary or unnecessary, and compare the two categories. Expenses such as rent, insurance and school materials are necessary, while costs like going to the movies, food delivery app purchases and extraneous streaming service subscriptions probably aren’t. Look at your balance of necessary vs. unnecessary purchases to see where you can cut spending. Make sure your necessary expenses are covered before making unnecessary purchases. Make sure your necessary expenses are covered before making unnecessary purchases. Better yet, aim to cut unnecessary costs entirely.


Download a Budgeting App

To save more money as a college student, consider downloading a budgeting app. Some popular budgeting apps include Mint, YNAB and Acorns. These tools offer helpful visuals to display your spending trends and help you save cash to meet various goals. By setting aside money for specific intentions, you can meet your monthly payments and save for the future. Along with saving for experiences like a vacation or large purchases like a downpayment on a home or car, budgeting apps help students store funds for recurring payments and emergencies.
