Learn how to build your retirement fund

22 December 2023, 09:30am


Factors that can help seniors build their retirement funds for elderlies

  • Contributing to a pension plan: Many countries have mandatory or voluntary pension plans that provide income to retirees based on their contributions and earnings during their working years.
  • Saving and investing: Saving a portion of one’s income and investing it in a diversified portfolio of assets can help grow one’s retirement funds over time. Some common investment vehicles include stocks, bonds, cryptos, mutual funds, exchange-traded funds (ETFs), and annuities.
  • Your eligibility for government support: Many governments offer various forms of support to seniors, such as social pensions, health care subsidies, tax credits, and housing assistance.
  • Working longer or part-time may be a viable option to increase their retirement funds and stay active and engaged.
  • Setting retirement goals and committing to them: Having a clear vision of how much income one needs in retirement, when to retire, and what kind of lifestyle one wants to have can help one stay motivated and focused on saving and investing for the future
  • Starting saving today: The earlier one starts saving for retirement, the more time one has to benefit from compound interest and grow one’s retirement fund. Even small amounts of savings can make a big difference over time
  • Automating savings: Setting up automatic transfers from one’s income to one’s retirement account can help one save consistently and avoid spending temptations. One can also increase the amount of savings gradually as one’s income rises
  • Creating more sources of income: Having multiple streams of income can help one boost one’s retirement fund and reduce the reliance on a single source. Some examples of income sources are part-time work, freelance projects, rental properties, dividends, royalties, and annuities
  • Seeking professional advice: Retirement planning can be complex and challenging, especially when it involves different financial products, tax implications, and legal issues. Therefore, it may be helpful to consult a financial planner, an accountant, or a lawyer who can provide personalized guidance and recommendations based on one’s goals, needs, and circumstances
