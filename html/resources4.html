What is Budgeting?

Budgeting is a financial management tool that helps individuals plan and allocate their money wisely. It involves creating a plan for how you will spend and save your income, ensuring that you meet your financial goals and cover your essential expenses. For high school kids like you, who are just starting to manage your finances, learning budgeting skills early on can be valuable for building responsible financial habits.


50/30/20 budget

It is a simple and popular budgeting approach that can serve as a helpful guideline for allocating income and/or your allowance.

  1. 50% for needs

    2. Allocate 50% of your income to cover essential needs such as housing, utilities, groceries, transportation, and other necessities. These are non-negotiable expenses that you must prioritize.

  2. 30% for wants

    3. Reserve 30% of your income for discretionary spending on wants and non-essential items. This category includes things like entertainment, dining out, hobbies, and other personal indulgences. It provides flexibility for enjoyment without compromising your financial stability.

  3. 20% for savings

    4. Dedicate 20% of your income to savings and debt repayment. This category helps you build an emergency fund, save for future goals (like education or a car), and manage any outstanding debts you may have. This ensures that you are working towards financial security and long-term financial health.
